Defining & Measuring Success

I measure progress against site KPIs, program maturation and attributed incremental revenue

  • Site KPIs: KPI changes quarter over quarter with seasonality evaluations if relevant
  • Program impact potential: Velocity & Reach
  • Impact on decision making: Winning & Learning
  • Incremental revenue: Dollars added by CRO

In 2024, every major KPI for UHOne increased in each quarter

This was the only time this had happened since UHOne migrated site analytics to Adobe Analytics in 2021, confirming that seasonality did not play a role

In addition to clear KPI improvements, program productivity metrics also rose dramatically in 2024

  • Variations tested: 44, +29% YoY
  • Visits: 1.9M visits, +103% YoY
  • Activity conversions: 226K

4 new ways of working helped us achieve that 29% increase in velocity

  1. Templatized targeting: URL formulas and multi-page activities led to higher traffic experiments that achieved statistical significance more quickly
  2. Added alignment: A more defined strategic planning process ensured effort was spent on desirable tasks, limiting churn
  3. Fast-follows: Post-hoc test deep-dives led to occasional fast-follow tweaks that turned losers into winners with limited effort
  4. Design templates: Templatized elements enabled quicker deisgn and development but also led to frequent re-use of winning elements

New targeting methodologies also set us up for expanded or more targeted reach in 2024 and going into 2025

  1. Templatized URL targeting: Templatized URL targeting enabled testing across multiple pages with the same layout, most notably sitewide testing
  2. Visits: New vs Return personalization planning began the process of behavioral personalization tests that targeted higher impact audiences with outsized intent

My efforts to target high traffic and impact locations and audiences with high contrast, hypothesis-driven tests resulted in a 61% learning rate. Coupled with my strategic approach, I achieved a 51% win rate, over 4 times industry average

Revenue Attribution & Reporting

I even developed a novel revenue attribution methodology

The foundational principle is that a 5% lift in the upper funnel conversion doesn't equal 5% more revenue, but there is a statistical correlation between every KPI for every test's targeting and revenue

Here's my process for revenue attribution in 3 simple steps:

  1. Correlate relevant KPIs and targeting with revenue (r = Delta RPV / Delta KPI)
  2. Multiply r by delta primary KPI during testing (Delta RPV)
  3. Multiply that figure by impacted visits
